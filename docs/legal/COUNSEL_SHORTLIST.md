# Privacy & EdTech Counsel Shortlist (Ontario)

> Confirm fit with at least 2 firms before retaining one. Public-sector / school-board procurement is its own niche — generalist startup lawyers will miss things.

## Must-have expertise

- PIPEDA (federal private-sector privacy law) compliance
- MFIPPA (Municipal Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act) — applies to Ontario district school boards as public bodies
- Contracting with Ontario school boards (vendor agreements, DPA negotiation, FOI carve-outs)
- Cross-border data flows (US-hosted infrastructure, even temporarily)
- Children's privacy / parental consent best practices (parallel: US COPPA — relevant if we expand)

## Shortlist (verify currency before contacting)

1. **INQ Law (Toronto)** — boutique, privacy-focused, Carole Piovesan. Has worked with EdTech founders.
2. **Borden Ladner Gervais (BLG)** — privacy and cybersecurity group; expensive but credible for public-sector RFPs.
3. **Osler, Hoskin & Harcourt** — top-tier privacy group (Adam Kardash). Overkill for seed stage; useful when raising.
4. **Privacy & Access Council of Canada (PACC) member directory** — find a sole practitioner for cost-effective initial review.
5. **Velocity / Communitech in-house referrals** — if accepted into either, free intro to vetted counsel.

## What to ask for in the first engagement (~5-10 billable hours)

- Review of [PRIVACY_POLICY.md](./PRIVACY_POLICY.md) and [TERMS_OF_SERVICE.md](./TERMS_OF_SERVICE.md) drafts.
- Validation of [PARENTAL_CONSENT.md](./PARENTAL_CONSENT.md) wording and flow.
- Custom [DPA_TEMPLATE.md](./DPA_TEMPLATE.md) tuned for Ontario boards.
- Memo on data residency obligations when storing student personal information processed for an Ontario public body.
- Memo on whether SUMMIT is a "service provider" or "joint controller" under MFIPPA and the implications.
